Prompting Guidance for University Departments

Effective prompting is key to getting useful results from AI. Below are examples from various department perspectives using an Ok/Good/Better format.

Admissions Department

Ok:
What are some ways to improve student recruitment?
Good:
What are five effective strategies to increase undergraduate applications from diverse backgrounds?
Better:
As a university admissions officer, I need to develop a targeted outreach plan to increase applications from first-generation college students in rural areas. What are the top 3 evidence-based strategies I should implement, and what specific tactics could I use for each strategy? Please provide examples of successful programs from other universities.

Financial Aid Department

Ok:
How can we help students with financial aid?
Good:
What are some ways to improve financial aid communication with students?
Better:
As a financial aid administrator, I need to create a communication plan to ensure students understand their aid packages and deadlines. What are the most effective channels (email, text, portal) for reaching different student populations, and what key messages should be included in each communication? Please provide a sample timeline for the academic year.

Registrar's Office

Ok:
How can we improve course registration?
Good:
What are some strategies to reduce registration bottlenecks?
Better:
As a university registrar, I need to optimize our course registration system to handle 10,000 students efficiently. What are the top 3 process improvements I should implement, considering both technical solutions and policy changes? Please provide examples of successful registration systems from other universities of similar size.

IT Services

Ok:
How can we improve campus IT?
Good:
What are some ways to enhance cybersecurity on campus?
Better:
As a university IT director, I need to develop a comprehensive cybersecurity strategy to protect student data and research information. What are the top 5 security measures I should prioritize, and what specific technologies or protocols should I implement for each? Please provide a roadmap for implementation over the next 12 months.

AI Glossary

Familiarize yourself with these important AI terms:

1. Artificial Intelligence (AI): The simulation of human intelligence processes by machines.
2. Machine Learning (ML): A subset of AI that involves training algorithms to learn from data.
3. Natural Language Processing (NLP): AI's ability to understand and generate human language.
4. Large Language Model (LLM): Advanced AI models trained on vast amounts of text data.
5. Prompt Engineering: The art of crafting effective input for AI models.
6. Hallucination: When AI generates false or misleading information.
7. Fine-tuning: Adapting a pre-trained model to specific tasks.
8. Embeddings: Numerical representations of text that capture semantic meaning.
9. Token: A unit of text (word or subword) processed by AI models.
10. Context Window: The amount of text an AI model can process at once.
11. Generative AI: AI that can create new content like text, images, or code.
12. Supervised Learning: Training AI with labeled data.
13. Unsupervised Learning: Training AI to find patterns in unlabeled data.
14. Reinforcement Learning: Training AI through trial and error with rewards.
15. Transfer Learning: Applying knowledge from one task to another.
16. Bias: Systematic errors in AI outputs due to flawed training data.
17. Overfitting: When AI performs well on training data but poorly on new data.
18. Underfitting: When AI is too simple to capture data patterns.
19. Inference: The process of generating predictions from a trained model.
20. Ground Truth: The objective reality used to train and evaluate AI models.
