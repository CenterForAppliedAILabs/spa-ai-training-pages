AI Prompting Fundamentals

The Ok/Good/Better Framework

This framework helps you improve your prompts from basic to excellent. Each level builds on the previous one, adding specificity and context to get better AI responses.

❌ OK: Basic Prompt

"Write a policy about remote work."

This prompt is too vague and will likely produce generic results that don't meet your specific needs.

⚠️ GOOD: Improved Prompt

"Write a policy about remote work for university staff. Include guidelines for productivity and communication."

This prompt provides more context but still lacks important details about your specific institution and requirements.

✅ BETTER: Excellent Prompt

"Write a comprehensive remote work policy for a mid-sized university's administrative staff. The policy should address eligibility criteria, equipment requirements, security protocols for handling student data, productivity expectations, communication guidelines, and compliance with FERPA regulations. Format it as a formal policy document suitable for department heads to implement."

This prompt includes specific context, requirements, format instructions, and compliance considerations for a tailored, actionable policy.

Key Prompting Principles

  • • Be specific about your role and context
  • • Include formatting requirements
  • • Specify the audience and purpose
  • • Mention any compliance or policy requirements
  • • Provide examples when helpful

Common Prompt Elements

  • • Role: "As a university registrar..."
  • • Task: "Create a workflow for..."
  • • Context: "For a student with..."
  • • Format: "In a table format..."
  • • Constraints: "Following FERPA guidelines..."

Department-Specific Prompting Guides

Below are tailored prompting examples for various university departments. Each section provides Ok/Good/Better examples to help you craft effective prompts.

1. Admissions Office

❌ OK: Basic
"Help me write an email to prospective students."
⚠️ GOOD: Improved
"Write an email to high school seniors about our university's application process and deadlines."
✅ BETTER: Excellent
"As the Director of Admissions at a mid-sized liberal arts university, create a personalized email template for high school seniors in their final semester. The email should highlight our rolling admission deadline (March 15), explain the application requirements, address common concerns about financial aid, and include a friendly tone that reflects our university's values of community and academic excellence. Format it with a clear subject line, greeting, 3-4 concise paragraphs, and a compelling call-to-action to schedule a campus visit."

2. Academic Affairs

❌ OK: Basic
"Create a syllabus for a course."
⚠️ GOOD: Improved
"Create a syllabus for an Introduction to Psychology course."
✅ BETTER: Excellent
"As an Associate Dean in the College of Arts and Sciences, develop a comprehensive syllabus for 'Introduction to Psychology' (PSY 101), a 3-credit undergraduate course. The syllabus should follow our university's template requirements, include learning outcomes aligned with APA guidelines, a detailed weekly schedule with required readings from our adopted textbook, assessment methods (exams, papers, participation), academic integrity policies, accommodations information, and contact details. Ensure it meets accessibility standards and includes clear expectations for both in-person and online components of this hybrid course."

3. Student Services

❌ OK: Basic
"Write a welcome message for new students."
⚠️ GOOD: Improved
"Write a welcome message for international students arriving on campus."
✅ BETTER: Excellent
"As the Director of International Student Services, create a comprehensive welcome guide for new international students arriving for the fall semester. The guide should include information about visa requirements, campus orientation schedule, housing check-in procedures, health insurance enrollment, local transportation options, emergency contacts, cultural adjustment tips, and important dates for the academic year. Format it as a printable PDF with clear sections, checklists for items to complete before arrival, and contact information for each department. Use a warm, supportive tone while providing practical, actionable information."

4. Registrar's Office

❌ OK: Basic
"Help with a transfer credit policy."
⚠️ GOOD: Improved
"Write a transfer credit evaluation policy for undergraduate students."
✅ BETTER: Excellent
"As the University Registrar, develop a comprehensive transfer credit evaluation policy for undergraduate students transferring from accredited institutions. The policy should specify maximum credit hours accepted, minimum grade requirements (C- or better), course equivalency determination process, credit limits for technical/vocational courses, articulation agreements with community colleges, and procedures for appealing credit decisions. Include clear guidelines for academic departments to follow when reviewing course syllabi and determining equivalencies. Format it as an official policy document with effective dates and approval signatures required."

5. Financial Aid Office

❌ OK: Basic
"Explain financial aid options."
⚠️ GOOD: Improved
"Create a guide for students about applying for financial aid."
✅ BETTER: Excellent
"As a Financial Aid Counselor, create a comprehensive guide for first-generation college students and their families about navigating the financial aid process. The guide should explain FAFSA completion step-by-step, differentiate between grants, scholarships, work-study, and loans, outline important deadlines, provide tips for maximizing aid eligibility, include a glossary of financial aid terms, and offer contact information for personalized assistance. Use plain language, include visual aids like checklists and timelines, and address common concerns about debt and repayment options. Format it as a user-friendly brochure that can be distributed at college fairs and during orientation."

6. Human Resources

❌ OK: Basic
"Write a job description."
⚠️ GOOD: Improved
"Write a job description for an administrative assistant position."
✅ BETTER: Excellent
"As the HR Manager for a university's College of Business, create a detailed job description for an Administrative Assistant position in the Dean's Office. The description should include: job title, department overview, reporting structure, essential duties (calendar management, travel arrangements, document preparation, phone coverage), required qualifications (education, experience, skills), preferred qualifications, physical requirements, work environment details, salary range, benefits summary, and equal opportunity statement. Ensure it complies with ADA requirements and reflects our commitment to diversity, equity, and inclusion. Use our university's standard format and include clear application instructions."

7. Research Administration

❌ OK: Basic
"Help with a grant proposal."
⚠️ GOOD: Improved
"Write a grant proposal for STEM education research."
✅ BETTER: Excellent
"As a Research Administrator, develop a comprehensive NSF grant proposal for a 3-year study on improving STEM retention rates for underrepresented minority students. The proposal should include: project summary, intellectual merit section with literature review and theoretical framework, broader impacts addressing diversity in STEM, detailed methodology with mixed-methods approach, timeline with milestones, detailed budget justification, facilities and resources description, and evaluation plan. Align with NSF review criteria and include letters of support from partnering community colleges. Format according to NSF guidelines with proper page limits and formatting requirements."

8. IT Services

❌ OK: Basic
"Create IT security guidelines."
⚠️ GOOD: Improved
"Write cybersecurity guidelines for faculty and staff."
✅ BETTER: Excellent
"As the IT Security Manager for a university with 15,000 students and 2,000 employees, create comprehensive cybersecurity guidelines for faculty and staff. The guidelines should cover: password management best practices, phishing email identification and reporting, secure handling of sensitive student data (FERPA compliance), VPN usage for remote access, secure file sharing protocols, device security for laptops and mobile devices, social media security, incident reporting procedures, and regular security training requirements. Include practical examples, red flags to watch for, and step-by-step instructions for common security tasks. Format as an accessible PDF with infographics and quick reference checklists."

9. Marketing and Communications

❌ OK: Basic
"Write content for our website."
⚠️ GOOD: Improved
"Write content for the university's admissions webpage."
✅ BETTER: Excellent
"As the Director of Marketing and Communications, create engaging content for the university's main admissions webpage targeting high-achieving high school juniors and their parents. The content should highlight our academic programs, campus life, student support services, career outcomes, and unique selling points. Include compelling headlines, student testimonials, statistics on job placement and graduate school acceptance, information about financial aid and scholarships, and clear calls-to-action for campus visits and applications. Ensure the tone is inspiring yet informative, SEO-optimized, and aligned with our brand voice. Provide content for hero section, program highlights, student life, and next steps sections."

10. Facilities Management

❌ OK: Basic
"Create a maintenance schedule."
⚠️ GOOD: Improved
"Create a preventive maintenance schedule for campus buildings."
✅ BETTER: Excellent
"As the Facilities Manager for a university campus with 50 buildings and 2 million square feet, develop a comprehensive preventive maintenance schedule for the academic year. The schedule should include: monthly HVAC system checks, quarterly fire safety inspections, semi-annual roof inspections, annual boiler maintenance, bi-annual window cleaning, regular landscaping schedules, and emergency preparedness checks. Include responsible departments, estimated time requirements, cost projections, and compliance requirements with ADA and building codes. Format as a detailed spreadsheet with Gantt chart visualization and integrate with our existing work order management system."

AI Glossary for Administrators

Essential terms and concepts you should know when working with AI tools.

A-D

AI (Artificial Intelligence):

Computer systems that can perform tasks typically requiring human intelligence, such as understanding language, recognizing patterns, and making decisions.

Algorithm:

A set of step-by-step instructions that a computer follows to solve a problem or complete a task.

Chatbot:

A computer program that simulates conversation with human users, especially over the internet.

Data Privacy:

The protection of personal information and ensuring it is collected, used, and stored appropriately.

Deep Learning:

A subset of machine learning that uses neural networks with many layers to learn from large amounts of data.

Digital Literacy:

The ability to use digital technology, communication tools, and networks to access, manage, integrate, evaluate, and create information.

E-H

Ethics:

Moral principles that guide the development and use of AI technologies, ensuring fairness and avoiding harm.

FERPA:

Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act - a federal law that protects the privacy of student education records.

GPT (Generative Pre-trained Transformer):

A type of AI model designed to generate human-like text based on the input it receives.

Hallucination:

When an AI generates false or misleading information as if it were true.

Human-AI Collaboration:

The partnership between humans and AI systems to achieve better outcomes than either could alone.

Innovation:

The process of introducing new ideas, methods, or products to improve existing systems or create new solutions.

I-L

Large Language Model (LLM):

A type of AI model trained on vast amounts of text data to understand and generate human-like language.

Machine Learning:

A subset of AI that enables computers to learn from data without being explicitly programmed.

Natural Language Processing (NLP):

The ability of computers to understand, interpret, and generate human language.

Neural Network:

A computer system modeled after the human brain that recognizes patterns and makes predictions.

OpenAI:

An artificial intelligence research laboratory and company known for developing ChatGPT and other AI technologies.

Prompt Engineering:

The practice of crafting effective instructions for AI systems to get desired outputs.

M-P

Prompt:

The input or question you give to an AI system to get a response.

Productivity:

The efficiency of achieving maximum output with minimum wasted effort or expense.

Quality Assurance:

The process of ensuring that AI-generated content meets required standards and accuracy.

Reliability:

The consistency and dependability of AI systems in producing accurate and trustworthy results.

Research Administration:

The management and oversight of research activities, including grants, compliance, and reporting.

Security:

Protecting AI systems and data from unauthorized access, use, disclosure, disruption, modification, or destruction.

R-Z

Training Data:

The information used to teach AI models how to perform tasks and make predictions.

User Experience (UX):

The overall experience a person has when interacting with AI tools and systems.

Validation:

The process of confirming that AI systems work correctly and produce accurate results.

Workflow:

A sequence of steps or processes that AI tools can help streamline and improve.

Zero-shot Learning:

When AI can perform tasks it wasn't specifically trained for, based on general understanding.

  • • Use when discussing AI implementation with IT teams
  • • Include in training materials for staff
  • • Share with colleagues to build common understanding
