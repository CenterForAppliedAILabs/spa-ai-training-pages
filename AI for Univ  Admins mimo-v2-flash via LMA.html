Getting Started with LLM Tools

Before diving into prompting strategies, familiarize yourself with these popular Large Language Model tools. Each has unique strengths for university administration tasks.

ChatGPT (OpenAI)

Best for: General administrative tasks, email drafting, policy interpretation

Access: chat.openai.com
Cost: Free tier available, Plus at $20/month

Excellent for creative tasks, document summarization, and conversational assistance. Strong at understanding context.

Claude (Anthropic)

Best for: Long document analysis, complex reasoning, policy review

Access: claude.ai
Cost: Free tier available, Pro at $20/month

Excels at analyzing large documents and maintaining conversation context. Strong safety features for institutional use.

Microsoft Copilot

Best for: Office integration, Excel formulas, PowerPoint creation

Access: copilot.microsoft.com
Cost: Included with Microsoft 365 (some features)

Seamlessly integrates with Office suite. Ideal for institutions already using Microsoft ecosystem.

Google Gemini

Best for: Research, Google Workspace integration, data analysis

Access: gemini.google.com
Cost: Free with Google account

Strong research capabilities and integration with Google Workspace. Good for fact-checking and data tasks.

The Ok/Good/Better Prompting Framework

Master the art of prompting by seeing how the same task can be approached at three different quality levels. This framework helps you understand what makes an effective prompt.

Understanding the Framework

OK

Basic prompt: Gets a response but may need clarification or revision. Often vague or missing context.

GOOD

Clear prompt: Provides specific context and format. Gets useful results with minimal editing.

BETTER

Optimized prompt: Includes role, context, constraints, examples, and format. Delivers production-ready results.

🏛️ Admissions Department

OK Draft an email to prospective students
Write an email about our university
GOOD Draft an email to prospective students
Write an email to prospective students about our university's spring open house event. Include details about campus tours, faculty meetings, and financial aid information. Keep it friendly and professional, around 200 words.
BETTER Draft an email to prospective students
You are an admissions counselor at a mid-sized private university. Write a personalized email to a high school junior who has shown interest in our STEM programs. The email should: Context: - Recipient: Sarah Chen, GPA 3.8, interested in Computer Science - Event: Spring Open House, March 15th, 2-6 PM - Previous contact: She downloaded our CS program brochure Requirements: - Warm, welcoming tone (not overly formal) - Highlight specific STEM facilities and opportunities - Mention the opportunity to meet with CS faculty - Include financial aid workshop information - Clear call-to-action to RSVP - Length: 180-220 words Format: Professional email with subject line, greeting, body paragraphs, and closing signature.

BETTER Plan orientation activities
You are the Director of New Student Orientation at a diverse university. Design a comprehensive 3-day orientation program for 800 incoming freshmen: Context: - Student body: 60% first-generation college students, 40% live on campus - International students: 15% from 30+ countries - Challenges: High anxiety about academics, social integration concerns - Goals: Build community, reduce anxiety, provide resources, ensure safety Requirements: 1. Create a detailed hour-by-hour schedule for Days 1-3 - Include specific activity names (not just "icebreaker") - Balance required vs. optional sessions - Build in meal times and breaks - Consider heat/weather (outdoor vs. indoor activities) 2. Add evaluation metrics: - 5 survey questions for participants - 3 metrics for success measurement Format: Professional program proposal with clear sections, actionable details, and contingency planning. Tone: Organized, student-centered, and thorough.

BETTER Write job description
You are an HR manager at a university hiring for an Administrative Assistant in Academic Affairs. Create a compelling, inclusive, and comprehensive job posting: Context: - Reports to: Associate Provost - Team: 8-person office supporting 400 faculty - Key functions: Faculty support, accreditation documentation, event coordination - Salary: $45,000-52,000 (commensurate with experience) - Benefits: Full benefits, tuition remission, professional development Requirements: 1. Job Title & Summary (1 paragraph) - Compelling opening that attracts diverse candidates - Clear indication of impact and purpose 2. Inclusion Statement (1 paragraph) - Commitment to diversity, equity, and inclusion - Encouragement of diverse candidates to apply 7. Application Instructions - Required documents (resume, cover letter) - Where to apply - Deadline - Timeline for hiring process Format: Professional job posting that balances detail with readability. Use inclusive language and emphasize institutional values. Make it scannable with clear sections.

BETTER Create security policy
You are the Chief Information Security Officer (CISO) at a university. Draft a comprehensive but user-friendly IT security policy for faculty and staff: Context: - 2,500 employees (faculty and staff) - Regulatory requirements: FERPA, potential HIPAA for health center - Recent phishing incidents prompted this policy - Need to balance security with usability - Previous policy was too technical and ignored Requirements: 1. Executive Summary (2-3 paragraphs) - Why security matters (student data protection, institutional reputation) - What's changed and why - Leadership commitment to support 2. Password Requirements (clear specifications) - Minimum length, complexity rules - Prohibited patterns - Multi-factor authentication (MFA) requirements - Password manager recommendations 3. Acceptable Use Policy (numbered list) - Personal device usage - Email and communication standards - Cloud storage approved services - Social media guidelines - Public Wi-Fi warnings 4. Threat Recognition (visual-friendly section) - How to identify phishing (3-5 specific red flags) - What to do if you suspect a breach (step-by-step) - Reporting procedures (who to contact, what info to include) 5. Consequences & Enforcement (paragraph) - Progressive discipline approach - Technical restrictions for non-compliance - Positive incentives for compliance 6. Training & Resources (bullet points) - Required annual training - Quick reference guide availability - IT support contact methods - FAQ section 7. Acknowledgment Form (template) - Digital signature requirement - Date of acknowledgment - Next review date Format: Policy document that's accessible to non-technical users. Use plain language, avoid jargon, include visual elements (✓, ✗, ⚠️). Create something people will actually read and follow.

🎯 Pro Tips for Better Prompts

  • Always specify your role: "You are a [position] at [institution type]"
  • Provide context: Audience size, institutional characteristics, regulatory environment
  • Define constraints: Word count, format, tone, deadline
  • Request specific formats: Tables, bullet points, numbered lists, templates
  • Include examples: When possible, provide sample text or structure
  • Iterate: Use follow-up prompts to refine and improve results
  • Always review: AI outputs need human verification for accuracy and appropriateness

AI Glossary for University Administrators

Essential terms and concepts you need to understand to effectively use AI in your administrative role.

Artificial Intelligence (AI)
Computer systems that can perform tasks typically requiring human intelligence, such as understanding language, recognizing patterns, and making decisions.
Large Language Model (LLM)
An AI system trained on vast amounts of text data to understand and generate human-like language. Examples: GPT-4, Claude, Gemini.
Prompt
The instructions or questions you provide to an AI system to generate a response. The quality of your prompt directly impacts the quality of the output.
Prompt Engineering
The practice of designing and optimizing prompts to get the best possible results from AI systems.
Context Window
The amount of text an AI model can consider at one time when generating responses. Larger windows allow for more detailed conversations.
Token
The basic unit of text that AI models process. Typically, 100 tokens ≈ 75 words. Models have token limits per conversation.
Temperature
A setting that controls how creative vs. predictable AI responses are. Lower = more focused, higher = more creative.
Hallucination
When an AI confidently states false information as fact. Always verify important information from AI systems.
Fine-tuning
Training a pre-existing AI model on specific data to improve its performance on particular tasks or domains.
Natural Language Processing (NLP)
The branch of AI focused on enabling computers to understand, interpret, and generate human language.
Chain of Thought
A prompting technique that asks the AI to show its reasoning step-by-step, often leading to more accurate results.
Few-Shot Learning
Providing the AI with a few examples of what you want in your prompt to guide its output.
Zero-Shot Learning
Asking the AI to perform a task without providing any examples, relying on its pre-training alone.
FERPA
Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act. Federal law protecting student education records. Critical consideration when using AI with student data.
Bias
Systematic unfairness in AI outputs that can reflect training data limitations. Important to recognize and mitigate in administrative contexts.
API (Application Programming Interface)
A way to access AI models programmatically rather than through a chat interface. Used for integrating AI into existing systems.
Generative AI
AI systems that create new content (text, images, code) rather than just analyzing existing data.
Ethical AI
The practice of developing and using AI systems in ways that are fair, transparent, accountable, and beneficial to society.
Model
The specific AI system or algorithm that processes inputs and generates outputs. Different models have different capabilities.
Multimodal
AI systems that can process and generate multiple types of content (text, images, audio) simultaneously.
Output
The response or content generated by an AI system based on your prompt. Always requires human review and editing.
Persona
Assigning a specific role or character to the AI in your prompt (e.g., "You are an experienced registrar") to guide response style and content.
System Prompt
Instructions given to the AI that define its behavior throughout a conversation. Sets the overall context and constraints.
