Prompting Guidance by Department

Learn how to craft effective prompts for different university departments using the Ok/Good/Better format. Click the copy button to copy any prompt to your clipboard.

Admissions Department

OK

Basic prompt:

Write a response to an applicant asking about admission requirements.
GOOD

Improved prompt:

Write a professional email response to a prospective student inquiring about undergraduate admission requirements for Fall 2024. Include information about GPA, test scores, and application deadlines.
BETTER

Optimal prompt:

You are an admissions counselor at a mid-sized university. Draft a warm, personalized email to a prospective student (Alex Chen) who is interested in our Computer Science program. Address their specific questions about GPA requirements (3.5+ preferred), test-optional policy, and the early action deadline of November 1st. Include a link to our virtual tour and offer to schedule a meeting with a CS faculty member. Tone: welcoming, professional, and encouraging. Keep it under 200 words.

Financial Aid Department

OK
Explain FAFSA to a parent.
GOOD
Create a clear explanation of the FAFSA process for parents of a first-year student, including key deadlines, required documents, and how aid is calculated.
BETTER
Act as a financial aid advisor. Create a comprehensive FAQ document for parents about the FAFSA. Include: 1) What is FAFSA and why it's important, 2) Step-by-step application process, 3) List of required documents (tax returns, W-2s, etc.), 4) Key deadlines (federal, state, and institutional), 5) How Expected Family Contribution (EFC) is calculated, 6) Common mistakes to avoid, 7) What to do if parents are divorced/separated, 8) Links to official resources. Format as a clear, scannable document with headings and bullet points. Use plain language avoiding financial jargon.

Human Resources Department

OK
Write a job description for an administrative assistant.
GOOD
Create a job description for an Administrative Assistant position at a university, including responsibilities, qualifications, and required skills.
BETTER
Generate a comprehensive job description for a Senior Administrative Assistant role in the Office of the Provost at a research university. Include: 1) Position summary highlighting the role's importance in academic leadership support, 2) Detailed responsibilities (calendar management, meeting coordination, confidential document handling, budget tracking), 3) Required qualifications (5+ years experience, Bachelor's degree preferred), 4) Technical skills (MS Office suite, database management, virtual meeting platforms), 5) Soft skills (discretion, communication, problem-solving), 6) Salary range ($45,000-$55,000), 7) Benefits information, 8) Equal opportunity employer statement. Use professional language and university-specific terminology.

Facilities Management Department

OK
Write a maintenance schedule.
GOOD
Create a preventive maintenance schedule for university buildings including HVAC, plumbing, and electrical systems.
BETTER
Act as a Facilities Management Director. Develop a comprehensive 12-month preventive maintenance plan for a medium-sized university campus (5 academic buildings, 3 dormitories, 2 dining halls). Include: 1) Monthly HVAC filter replacement schedule, 2) Quarterly plumbing inspections, 3) Annual electrical system testing, 4) Seasonal landscaping tasks, 5) Emergency response protocols, 6) Vendor management guidelines, 7) Budget allocation recommendations ($150,000 annual budget), 8) Key performance indicators for tracking. Format as a table with months, tasks, responsible parties, and estimated costs. Consider academic calendar to minimize disruption.

Academic Affairs Department

OK
Create a course syllabus template.
GOOD
Generate a comprehensive syllabus template for undergraduate courses that meets accreditation standards.
BETTER
You are the Director of Academic Affairs. Create a master syllabus template for undergraduate courses that complies with Higher Learning Commission (HLC) accreditation standards and includes all required institutional elements. Structure should include: 1) Course identification (code, title, credits, semester), 2) Instructor information with office hours and contact methods, 3) Detailed course description and learning objectives aligned with Bloom's taxonomy, 4) Required textbooks and materials with ISBNs, 5) Grading policy breakdown (percentages for assignments, exams, participation), 6) Weekly schedule with topics and readings, 7) Assignment descriptions with rubrics, 8) Academic integrity policy, 9) Disability services statement, 10) Diversity and inclusion statement, 11) Communication expectations, 12) Emergency procedures. Include placeholders for customization and notes for faculty on adapting the template. Format as a professional document that can be easily edited.

Prompting Best Practices

  • Be specific about the role you want the AI to play
  • Include context and background information
  • Specify format, length, and tone
  • Provide examples when possible
  • Break complex tasks into smaller steps
  • Iterate and refine your prompts based on results

Getting Started with Popular LLM Chat Tools

An overview of popular Large Language Model tools that university administrators can use, with setup instructions and best practices.

OpenAI ChatGPT

The most widely recognized AI chatbot, suitable for a variety of administrative tasks.

Setup Instructions:

  1. Visit chat.openai.com
  2. Create an account with your university email
  3. Choose ChatGPT Plus ($20/month) for GPT-4 access
  4. Review OpenAI's data privacy policies

Best For:

  • Drafting emails and communications
  • Brainstorming ideas
  • Summarizing documents
  • General administrative tasks

Microsoft Copilot (formerly Bing Chat)

Integrated with Microsoft 365, making it ideal for universities already using Microsoft products.

Setup Instructions:

  1. Visit copilot.microsoft.com
  2. Sign in with your Microsoft account (university Azure AD if available)
  3. Enable commercial data protection for institutional use
  4. Access via Edge browser or mobile app

Best For:

  • Excel data analysis
  • PowerPoint creation
  • Word document editing
  • Teams meeting summaries

Google Gemini (formerly Bard)

Google's AI assistant, particularly useful if your institution uses Google Workspace.

Setup Instructions:

  1. Visit gemini.google.com
  2. Sign in with Google account
  3. Check if your institution has Google Workspace for Education
  4. Review Google's privacy settings for educational use

Best For:

  • Integration with Google Docs/Sheets
  • Research and fact-checking
  • Image generation (with Gemini Advanced)
  • Email composition in Gmail

Anthropic Claude

Known for strong reasoning capabilities and longer context windows, good for complex document analysis.

Setup Instructions:

  1. Visit claude.ai
  2. Create an account
  3. Consider Claude Pro for advanced features
  4. Review Anthropic's privacy policy for institutional use

Best For:

  • Long document analysis (up to 200K tokens)
  • Complex reasoning tasks
  • Policy review and analysis
  • Data interpretation

Specialized Academic Tools

AI tools specifically designed for educational institutions.

Examples:

  • Elicit: Research assistant for literature reviews
  • Consensus: AI search engine for scientific research
  • Gradescope: AI-assisted grading (STEM focused)
  • Turnitin: AI writing detection integrated with plagiarism checking

Security & Privacy Considerations

  • Never input: Student PII, financial data, or protected health information
  • Check institutional policies: Many universities have approved AI tool lists
  • Data retention: Understand how each platform stores and uses your data
  • Commercial terms: Look for enterprise/education agreements
  • Output verification: Always fact-check AI-generated content

Quick Start Recommendation

For most university administrators, start with Microsoft Copilot (if your institution uses Microsoft 365) or ChatGPT for general tasks. Both offer strong privacy protections and are widely accepted in educational settings.
