Getting Started with AI Tools

Modern Large Language Models (LLMs) function as highly sophisticated "reasoning engines" rather than simple search engines.

ChatGPT (OpenAI)

The industry pioneer. Best for versatile reasoning, data analysis, and general content creation.

  • Step 1: Visit chatgpt.com
  • Step 2: Sign up with your university email.
  • Step 3: Start with simple requests to "get a feel" for the conversational flow.
Best for: Productivity

Claude (Anthropic)

Known for "Constitutional AI." Excellent for processing large documents and writing in a human-like tone.

  • Step 1: Visit claude.ai
  • Step 2: Upload PDFs (Syllabi, Handbooks) to analyze them instantly.
  • Step 3: Use the "Projects" feature to organize your department notes.
Best for: Document Analysis

Gemini (Google)

Integrated with Google Workspace. Great for real-time information retrieval and integration with Docs/Sheets.

  • Step 1: Access via gemini.google.com
  • Step 2: Use the "@" feature to pull in data from your Google Drive.
  • Step 3: Ideal for cross-referencing campus schedules and emails.
Best for: Google Ecosystem

The Art of Prompting

Mastering AI is about moving from "Ask" to "Instruct." We use the Ok/Good/Better framework to show you the difference.

1. Admissions Department

Ok

"Write an email to a prospective student about our nursing program."

Result: Generic, likely misses specific US University value propositions.

Good

"Write a professional email to a prospect interested in the RN to BSN program. Mention that it is flexible and online."

Result: Better, but still lacks a call to action and "voice."

Better (Professional)

"Act as a Senior Admissions Counselor. Write a warm, encouraging email to a prospect named [Name] for our RN to BSN program. Structure: 1. Acknowledge their commitment to nursing. 2. Highlight our flexible 8-week courses. 3. Invite them to a Zoom info session this Tuesday. Tone: Professional yet empathetic."

2. Registrar's Office

Ok

"Explain transfer credit rules."

Good

"Summarize this PDF policy on transfer credits into 5 bullet points for a student."

Better (Professional)

"Analyze the attached Transfer Credit Policy. Create a 'Frequently Asked Questions' document for staff training. Focus on: Residency requirements, time limits for science credits, and the process for appealing a credit denial. Use a professional table format."

3. Student Services & Success

Ok

"How can students study better?"

Good

"Create a 1-week study plan for a student who is struggling with time management."

Better (Professional)

"Create a 'First-Year Success Resource Guide'. Focus on students who are working full-time. Include: 1. Strategies for reading academic journals quickly. 2. A checklist for contacting their advisor. 3. Three mindfulness techniques for exam stress. Use a supportive, coaching tone."

4. IT & Data Privacy

Ok

"Write an AI policy."

Good

"Draft a memo to faculty about using AI in the classroom safely."

Better (Professional)

"Draft a professional memo to University Staff regarding AI Privacy Guidelines. Address: 1. Why inputting student PII (Personally Identifiable Information) into public AI is a FERPA violation. 2. Approved university AI tools. 3. Whom to contact for a security review of a new tool. Make it clear, authoritative, and helpful."
