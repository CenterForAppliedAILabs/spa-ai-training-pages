Welcome to the AI Revolution in Higher Education

Artificial Intelligence is transforming higher education administration, offering unprecedented opportunities to improve efficiency, personalize student services, and optimize institutional operations. This course is specifically designed for university administrators with no prior AI experience.

You'll learn practical skills to immediately apply in your work, including how to effectively use Large Language Models (LLMs) like ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini for administrative tasks across various university departments.

Getting Started with Popular LLM Chat Tools

Large Language Models (LLMs) are AI systems trained on vast amounts of text data that can understand and generate human-like text. For university administrators, these tools can be invaluable assistants for drafting communications, analyzing data, generating reports, and much more.

ChatGPT (OpenAI)

One of the most popular and accessible LLMs with a free tier available. Excellent for general administrative tasks.

  • Free version available
  • Web interface and mobile app
  • File upload capabilities

Getting Started Tips

  1. Start with the free versions - All major platforms offer free tiers to experiment with
  2. Create an account - Sign up with your university email when possible
  3. Experiment with simple prompts - Begin with basic tasks like summarizing emails or drafting responses
  4. Review privacy policies - Be mindful of sharing sensitive student or institutional data
  5. Use clear, specific instructions - The quality of output depends heavily on how you phrase your requests

Prompting Guidance from Various Department Perspectives

Learn the "Ok/Good/Better" framework for crafting effective AI prompts. Each example shows how to improve prompts for specific administrative tasks.

Admissions Office

OK

Basic Prompt

A vague request that will produce generic results.

Write an email to prospective students.

Too vague, won't address specific needs

GOOD

Improved Prompt

More specific with some context about the audience.

Write an email to high school seniors who have been accepted to our university but haven't committed yet. Encourage them to choose our school.

Specific audience and goal

BETTER

Optimal Prompt

Comprehensive with tone, key points, and specific call-to-action.

As the Admissions Director, write a warm, encouraging email to high school seniors who have been accepted to State University but haven't committed yet. Mention: 1) Our 92% first-year retention rate, 2) Recent campus renovations including the new science building, 3) Scholarship opportunities still available, 4) Upcoming admitted student events. Use a professional but enthusiastic tone. Include a clear call-to-action to visit our commitment portal. Keep it under 300 words.

Specific tone, key points, length guidance

Clear role and call-to-action

Student Affairs

OK

Basic Prompt

Too broad without specific context or goals.

Create a student newsletter.

No context about content or audience

GOOD

Improved Prompt

Adds some specific topics and timeframe.

Create a weekly student newsletter for October with information about campus events and resources.

Includes timeframe and general topics

BETTER

Optimal Prompt

Comprehensive with specific sections, tone, and formatting.

Create a weekly student newsletter for the first week of October 2023 targeted at undergraduate students. Include these sections: 1) Upcoming campus events (include at least 5 events with dates/times), 2) Mental health resources available during midterms, 3) Spotlight on the Career Center's resume workshop, 4) Campus dining specials for the week. Use an engaging, friendly tone appropriate for college students. Format with clear headings and bullet points. Include a closing reminder about fall break dates.

Specific sections, tone, and formatting

Clear target audience and timeframe

Academic Affairs

OK

Basic Prompt

Lacks specific criteria and context about the course.

Write a course description.

No details about subject, level, or requirements

GOOD

Improved Prompt

Includes course subject and some basic elements.

Write a course description for an introductory data science course for undergraduate students.

Specifies subject and audience

BETTER

Optimal Prompt

Detailed with specific requirements, learning outcomes, and formatting.

Write a comprehensive course description for "Introduction to Data Science" (course code: DS-101), a 3-credit undergraduate course required for Computer Science majors. Include: 1) Course overview explaining what data science is and why it's important, 2) 5-6 specific learning outcomes starting with action verbs (e.g., "Students will be able to..."), 3) Prerequisites (STAT-201 and CS-110), 4) Brief weekly topic outline covering data collection, cleaning, visualization, and basic machine learning, 5) Required textbooks and software (Python, Jupyter Notebooks). Use formal academic tone. Format with clear headings.

Detailed structure with specific elements

Clear tone and formatting requirements

Finance & Administration

OK
Budget Justification

Write a budget request.

GOOD
Improved

Write a budget justification for new computer equipment for the library.

BETTER
Optimal

As the Library Director, write a detailed budget justification request for $25,000 to purchase 15 new computer workstations for the student computer lab. Include: 1) Current equipment age and performance issues, 2) Student demand data showing 90% occupancy during peak hours, 3) Specific models being requested with cost breakdown, 4) Expected benefits for student success and research capabilities, 5) Plan for disposing of old equipment. Use formal, persuasive language suitable for university administration review.

Research Office

OK
Grant Proposal

Write a grant proposal.

GOOD
Improved

Write a grant proposal for STEM education research.

BETTER
Optimal

Draft the project narrative section for an NSF grant proposal titled "Integrating AI Literacy Across Undergraduate STEM Curricula." The proposal seeks $500,000 over 3 years to develop and implement AI modules in biology, chemistry, and physics courses at mid-sized public universities. Include: 1) Intellectual merit discussing pedagogical innovation, 2) Broader impacts on workforce development and equity in STEM, 3) Specific project activities with timeline, 4) Assessment plan using mixed methods. Use formal academic language with clear headings. Reference recent NSF priority areas.

Copy & Paste Prompt Templates

Ready-to-use prompt templates for common university administrative tasks. Click the copy button to use them in your preferred LLM tool.

Meeting Agenda Generator

Generate a detailed meeting agenda with time allocations.

Create a 60-minute meeting agenda for [Department Name]'s monthly staff meeting. Include: 1) 5-minute welcome and updates, 2) 15-minute review of key metrics from last month, 3) 20-minute discussion on [Specific Topic], 4) 10-minute action item assignments, 5) 5-minute wrap-up and next steps. Format with clear time allocations for each section.

Policy Document Draft

Draft a clear, comprehensive policy document.

Draft a university policy on [Policy Topic] that includes: 1) Purpose and scope statement, 2) Definitions of key terms, 3) Specific procedures and requirements, 4) Roles and responsibilities, 5) Compliance and enforcement measures, 6) Effective date and review cycle. Use formal policy language with clear numbering for sections and subsections.

Student Email Response

Craft a professional, helpful response to student inquiries.

Write a professional yet warm email response to a student inquiring about [Specific Issue]. Acknowledge their concern, provide clear information about [Relevant Policy/Procedure], outline next steps they should take, and include contact information for further assistance. Maintain a supportive tone while being clear about university policies.

Data Analysis Request

Analyze institutional data and provide insights.

Analyze the following dataset of [Type of Data, e.g., student enrollment figures, course evaluations] from the past 5 years. Identify: 1) Key trends and patterns, 2) Notable changes year-over-year, 3) Potential areas of concern, 4) Opportunities for improvement. Present findings in bullet points with brief explanations for each insight. If appropriate, suggest 2-3 actionable recommendations based on the analysis.
