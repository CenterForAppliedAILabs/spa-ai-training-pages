Overview

The Evolving Art of Prompting

Prompt engineering is shifting from "magic spells" to systems engineering. It is no longer about tricking the model, but about providing clear, structured context and constraints.

Guiding Principles

  • Context > Capability: Models are smart enough; they usually fail because they lack context, not intelligence.
  • Structure is King: Use XML tags, Markdown, or JSON schemas to separate instructions from data.
  • Iteration: The first prompt is never the final prompt.

The Goal

To move from "Chatting" to "Programmatic Control".

Target: Intermediate/Advanced
Prerequisite: Basic Chat familiarity
Strategy

Structural Frameworks

Advanced prompting requires breaking tasks down. We use mental models to guide the AI's reasoning process.

1. Few-Shot Prompting

Providing examples (shots) inside the prompt to guide the style and format of the output.

few-shot-example.txt
Task: Classify customer sentiment. Example 1: Input: "I waited 30 minutes and the food was cold." Output: Negative // Speed, Quality Example 2: Input: "Great service, but a bit pricey." Output: Mixed // Service, Price Input: "The interface is intuitive and fast." Output:

2. Chain of Thought (CoT)

Forcing the model to "show its work" before giving a final answer. This drastically reduces logic errors and hallucinations.

The "Magic" Phrase:
"Take a deep breath and think step-by-step."
(Or structurally: "Output your reasoning in a <thinking> tag before your final answer.")
Advanced Technique

Meta-Prompting & Improvers

The best prompt engineer is often the AI itself. Meta-prompting involves asking an AI to write or improve a prompt for another AI.

The Concept

Instead of struggling to find the right words, describe your *intent* to the AI and ask it to generate the optimal system instructions.

The Workflow

  1. Draft a basic request.
  2. Feed it to a "Prompt Improver".
  3. Use the improved prompt for the actual task.

Architecture

Systemic Prompting & Delimiters

As prompts get longer, AI models get confused about which part is instruction and which part is data. We use Delimiters to solve this.

Using XML Tags

Modern models (Gemini 3 Pro LMC, Claude 3.5, GPT-4) are trained to recognize XML-style tags as structural boundaries.

system-prompt.md
You are a financial analyst. Analyze the data provided in the <data> tags. Follow the criteria in the <criteria> tags. <criteria> - Focus on ROI and EBITDA. - Be concise. </criteria> <data> [Paste messy CSV data here] </data>

Role-Based Persona

Don't just say "You are an expert." Be specific. Use the CO-STAR method:

  • Context (Background info)
  • Objective (What needs to be done)
  • Style (Tone, voice)
  • Tone (Emotional resonance)
  • Audience (Who is reading)
  • Response Format (JSON, Markdown, Table)
